# Wagon Bones — Gameplay Suggestions & New Mechanics

## Executive Summary

The core tension in your game is: **dice are generic** (no suits, no fixed values like a card deck), so the "deck building" angle of Balatro is replaced by dice collection management + spent-pool cycling. That's a great start, but there's room to lean *way harder* into what dice can do that cards can't — physicality, probability manipulation, and the thematic richness of an Oregon Trail journey.

Below are ideas organized by category. Not all of these should be implemented — pick the ones that spark joy.

---

## 1. WEATHER SYSTEM (New Meta-Mechanic)

**The Big Idea:** Each leg of the journey has a randomized weather condition that modifies gameplay for the entire leg. This replaces the static "increasing difficulty" with something more dynamic and thematic.

**Weather Types:**
- **Clear Skies** — No effect (baseline)
- **Rainstorm** — All dice results are reduced by 1 (min 1). Iron dice are immune.
- **Blizzard** — Hand size reduced by 2, but all scored dice give +10 bonus miles (huddling together)
- **Drought** — Rerolls cost $1 each. Trail Rations equipment becomes incredibly valuable.
- **Dust Devil** — After rolling, 2 random dice swap values with each other
- **Heat Wave** — Diamond dice crack on ANY score (not just 25%). Gold dice melt (destroyed after scoring). But fire aura gives x2.5 instead of x1.5.
- **Thunderstorm** — 1 in 4 chance each day that a random die is "struck" and gains a random enhancement
- **Fog** — First 3 dice rolled each day are hidden until you commit to score (grime-lite)

**Why it works:** Gives each run a different flavor, forces adaptation, and is deeply thematic. Players can build around weather (stockpile iron dice for rainstorms, etc).

---

## 2. WAGON UPGRADES (Persistent Progression Layer)

**The Big Idea:** Between legs (at landmarks), you can spend money to upgrade your wagon. These are like permanent passive buffs that persist for the run — a third "build" axis alongside equipment and dice.

**Upgrade Slots (max 3-4):**
- **Reinforced Axle** — +1 hand size permanently
- **Covered Top** — Weather effects are halved
- **Grease Bucket** — First reroll each day is free
- **Extra Wheel** — If you would bust (fail a round), get 1 bonus day
- **Iron Plating** — Dice can't be destroyed by boss effects
- **Hidden Compartment** — +1 consumable slot
- **Lantern Hook** — Can see the next day's "draw" before picking dice
- **Water Barrel** — +$2 at end of each round (reliable income)

**Why it works:** Adds a long-term investment decision. Do you save money for the shop or invest in wagon infrastructure? Creates "wagon builds" as another strategic layer.

---

## 3. TRAIL EVENTS (Between-Round Narrative Moments)

**The Big Idea:** After beating a leg but before the shop, a random trail event occurs. These are choose-your-own-adventure moments with risk/reward.

**Examples:**
- **"River Crossing"** — Pay $5 to ford safely, or risk it: 50% chance nothing happens, 30% lose a random die, 20% find a rare equipment
- **"Broken Wheel"** — Lose 1 day next round OR pay $8 to fix immediately
- **"Fellow Traveler"** — A stranger offers to trade: one of your equipment for a random uncommon+ equipment (sight unseen)
- **"Abandoned Wagon"** — Gain 3 random unenhanced dice, but next boss is upgraded (harder)
- **"Gold Strike!"** — Spend your next 2 rerolls (next round) to gain $15 now
- **"Sick Oxen"** — Lose $3 per day next round, OR sacrifice a die to cure them
- **"Native Guide"** — Offers a free trail guide, but you must skip the next shop
- **"Bandit Ambush"** — Lose half your money OR sacrifice an equipment to fight them off (gain $10 bounty)

**Why it works:** Adds narrative variety, tough decisions, and makes each run feel like a *story*. Deeply thematic. Also breaks up the rhythm of play→shop→play→shop.

---

## 4. COMPANION SYSTEM (New Equipment Sub-Type)

**The Big Idea:** Companions are a special equipment type that levels up over time based on conditions. They take an equipment slot but grow stronger.

**Companions:**
- **Faithful Hound** — Starts at +2 mult. Gains +1 mult every time you score a full house. (Max +15)
- **Pack Mule** — Starts with +1 hand size. Every 3 legs survived, gain another +1 hand size. (Max +3 total)
- **Scout Eagle** — Starts by revealing 1 hidden/grime die. Every 2 bosses defeated, reveals 1 more.
- **Lucky Cat** — Starts at 1-in-6 chance to double money earned from scoring. Improves to 1-in-4, then 1-in-3 as you use supply cards.
- **Ghost Horse** — Starts at +20 miles per round. Loses 5 miles each time you reroll. Resets each leg.
- **Oxen Team** — x1 mult. Gains x0.1 for each day you DON'T use all your rerolls. Resets if you bust.

**Why it works:** Creates attachment, rewards long-term play patterns, and adds "leveling up" satisfaction. Very thematic — your animal companions grow more useful on the trail.

---

## 5. DICE CRAFTING / COMBINING

**The Big Idea:** Two dice can be fused at a "Blacksmith" shop (appears every other leg) to create a more powerful hybrid die.

**Rules:**
- Both dice are destroyed, one new die is created
- The new die inherits the enhancement of the LEFT die and the sticker of the RIGHT die
- If both have auras, you pick which one to keep
- The new die gets +1 to its base value permanently (a "forged" bonus)
- Special combo recipes exist:

**Recipes:**
- **Bone + Bone** = "Skull Die" — +8 mult instead of +4, but -10 miles
- **Iron + Iron** = "Steel Die" — x2 mult held in hand instead of x1.5
- **Gold + Gold** = "Platinum Die" — Earn $5 when scored instead of just end-of-round money  
- **Wood + Wood** = "Petrified Die" — +25 base miles and counts as both wood AND stone
- **Lucky + Lucky** = "Miracle Die" — Always triggers the lucky effect (guaranteed)
- **Diamond + Diamond** = "Flawless Die" — x2 mult, never cracks
- **Stone + Stone** = "Monolith Die" — 100 base miles, can never be destroyed or enhanced further

**Why it works:** Gives you something to DO with duplicate/weak dice. Creates exciting combo discovery moments. Adds a meaningful choice: keep two okay dice or fuse into one great die?

---

## 6. BOUNTY SYSTEM (Alternate Win Condition Per Round)

**The Big Idea:** Each round, in addition to the mile target, there's an optional "Bounty" — a specific challenge that gives bonus rewards if completed.

**Examples:**
- "Score using only pairs this round" — Reward: $10 + random trail guide
- "Never use more than 1 reroll per day" — Reward: random frontier encounter card
- "Score at least one 5-straight this round" — Reward: +1 equipment slot for this run
- "Beat this leg with 2 or fewer days" — Reward: $15
- "Score with every die in your collection this round" — Reward: random aura on a die
- "Don't score any 6s this round" — Reward: supply card + trail guide

**Why it works:** Gives experienced players optional challenges to chase. Rewards varied play instead of always doing the same optimal thing. Creates exciting "can I pull this off?" moments.

---

## 7. NEW DICE ENHANCEMENTS

- **Cursed Die** — x2 mult when scored, but your next reroll costs $2. Risk/reward.
- **Magnetic Die** — When rolled, pulls the values of adjacent dice 1 closer to its value. (If magnetic shows 4, dice next to it get +1 or -1 toward 4)
- **Explosive Die** — If it rolls a 6 (or 12 on d12), it scores itself AND destroys itself, giving x3 mult that hand. Can be recreated via supply card.
- **Phantom Die** — Doesn't go to spent pile when scored. But has a 1-in-3 chance to vanish forever each time it scores.
- **Twin Die** — Always created in pairs. Both must be scored in the same hand to activate their bonus (+20 miles each). If separated, they're just normal dice.
- **Rusted Die** — Costs $0 in shop. -5 miles when scored. But can be "restored" by using it in 5 hands, becoming a random rare enhancement.
- **Weighted Die** — Always rolls 1 higher than it would have (cap at max). Takes up 2 "slots" in your hand.
- **Hollow Die** — Worth 0 base miles/value, but copies the enhancement effect of the die to its left when scored.

## 8. NEW EQUIPMENT IDEAS

### Thematic / Oregon Trail Flavored:
- **Ox Yoke** — $5 — Dice scored in ascending order (left to right) give +5 miles each. Descending order gives +5 mult each.
- **Pemmican** — $4 — At the start of each day, if you have fewer than 3 dice in your pouch, refresh all spent dice automatically.
- **Wagon Tongue** — $6 — The first and last die scored each hand are retriggered.
- **Chimney Rock Charm** — $7 — If your score this hand would EXACTLY meet the target (within 10%), gain x2 mult bonus.
- **Jerky Strips** — $3 — +1 mult for each day remaining when you beat the round (rewards efficiency)
- **Caulked Wagon** — $5 — Immune to the next boss's negative effect. Breaks after use. Repairs at each shop for $3.
- **Sundial** — $6 — On the first day of each round, get +2 bonus rerolls.
- **Fence Post** — $4 — +4 mult per stone die scored this hand.
- **Flour Sack** — $3 — When you score fewer than 3 dice in a hand, gain $1 per unscored die.
- **Tanned Hide** — $5 — Dice that roll their maximum value (6 or 12) get +15 mult.
- **Whittling Knife** — $6 — At end of each round, choose 1 die to permanently increase its min roll by 1 (makes it impossible to roll below that value).
- **Sextant** — $7 — You can see the top 3 dice in your pouch before picking.

### Mechanical / Weird:
- **Mirror Dice** — $6 — If you score the same hand type twice in a row, second time gives x2 mult.
- **Rattlesnake Boots** — $5 — When a die rolls a 1, ALL 1s in your hand become 6s.
- **The Alamo** — $8 — If this is your final day and you haven't met the target, gain x5 mult for this hand only. (Legendary)
- **Dusty Ledger** — $4 — Earn $1 every time you cycle through your entire dice collection.
- **Hangman's Noose** — $7 — Destroy the lowest-value scored die each hand. Gain its value × $2.
- **Snake Charmer** — $5 — Pairs where both dice are the same enhancement give x2 mult.
- **Pioneer Spirit** — $6 — +3 mult for each UNIQUE enhancement type in your collection.
- **Undertaker's Box** — $8 — Every die that's been destroyed this run adds +2 permanent mult to this card.

## 9. NEW SUPPLY CARDS

- **Dust Storm** — Randomize the values of 3 random dice in your collection (reroll their enhancements)
- **Snake Bite** — Destroy 1 die, gain its enhancement on 2 other random dice
- **Lightning Strike** — Pick 1 die: it permanently gains +3 to its roll value (can exceed 6/12)
- **Fool's Gold** — Gain $15, but 2 random dice become "cursed" (worth -5 miles until cured)
- **Horseshoe Nail** — Pick 1 piece of equipment: it can't be destroyed or disabled for the rest of the run (becomes "eternal")
- **Tall Tale** — Copy the text of one equipment card onto another (stacks effects) for the current round only
- **Forked Path** — Look at the next 2 trail events. Pick one to happen now, skip the other entirely.
- **Snake Eyes Brew** — All your dice roll 1s this hand. (Powerful with "all 1s" equipment builds)

## 10. NEW FRONTIER ENCOUNTERS

- **The Wendigo** — Destroy 3 random dice. All remaining dice gain +1 permanent base miles for each die destroyed this run.
- **Haunted Campfire** — One random equipment becomes "Haunted" — it works backwards (mult becomes negative, miles become negative) but gives x3 mult. Sell it to remove the curse.
- **The Ferryman** — Pay $10. Choose any die to become ANY enhancement of your choice. (Targeted transformation)
- **Thunderbird** — All dice with aura have their aura doubled (holy = x2.25, fire = +20, ice = +100). One random die loses its aura.
- **Gold Vein** — All gold dice in your collection permanently gain fire aura. Lose $5 per gold die.

## 11. META-MECHANIC: "TRAIL REPUTATION"

**The Big Idea:** As you play, you build reputation that carries across legs. Reputation unlocks passive bonuses and changes shop offerings.

**How it works:**
- Gain reputation for: beating legs under par (fewer days), completing bounties, not using rerolls, scoring big hands
- Lose reputation for: busting, using too many rerolls, selling equipment

**Reputation Tiers:**
- **Unknown** (0-5) — No effect
- **Greenhorn** (6-15) — Shops offer 1 extra item
- **Trailhand** (16-30) — Supply cards are 15% cheaper
- **Pioneer** (31-50) — Trail events have a "good outcome" bonus option
- **Legend** (51+) — Unlock a special legendary equipment offer each shop

**Why it works:** Rewards skillful play without being punishing. Adds a "prestige" feeling. Creates interesting decisions (do I use this reroll and lose rep, or gamble on keeping it?).

---

## 12. "SCOUTING" PHASE (New Between-Day Mechanic)

**The Big Idea:** Before picking dice each day, you can "scout ahead" — spend a reroll to peek at what your next day's draw would look like, OR see what the weather/event will be tomorrow.

This makes rerolls a dual-purpose resource: information OR re-randomization. Creates interesting tension.

---

## 13. DICE "WEAR" SYSTEM

**The Big Idea:** Dice degrade over time, gaining "wear" each time they score. At 5 wear, they crack and become less reliable. At 10, they shatter (destroyed). You can repair dice at shops.

- **0-4 wear:** Normal
- **5-7 wear:** "Worn" — 1 in 6 chance to roll 1 lower than intended
- **8-9 wear:** "Cracked" — 1 in 3 chance to roll 1 lower
- **10 wear:** Destroyed

**Repair costs:** $1 per wear removed. Full repair = $5-10 depending on die quality.

**Why it works:** Creates resource management tension. Do you keep using your best die and risk it breaking? Or rotate through weaker dice to preserve the good ones? Synergizes beautifully with your existing spent-pool cycling mechanic.

**Counter-argument:** Might feel bad/punishing. Could be opt-in via a specific profession or game mode.

---

## 14. "FORMATION" BONUSES (Positional Scoring)

**The Big Idea:** The ORDER you place dice when scoring matters.

- **Ascending (1-2-3-4-5):** +20 bonus miles
- **Descending (5-4-3-2-1):** +5 bonus mult
- **Palindrome (1-3-5-3-1):** x1.5 mult
- **All Same:** Already covered by 5-of-a-kind, but could give bonus
- **Bookends (matching first and last):** +10 miles

**Why it works:** Adds a spatial puzzle element unique to dice games. Cards in Balatro don't have meaningful ordering in hand — this is something only dice can do well. Creates an additional micro-decision each hand.

---

## 15. DIFFICULTY MODIFIERS / "CURSES" (Endgame Variety)

After beating the game once, unlock modifiers for harder runs:

- **Manifest Destiny** — Miles required are 50% higher, but shop prices are halved
- **Cholera** — Lose 1 random die every 2 legs. Gain +$5 each time.
- **The Long Way Round** — 12 legs instead of 8, but bosses repeat with harder effects
- **Minimalist** — Start with 4 dice instead of normal amount. Equipment slots are doubled.
- **Jackpot or Bust** — Only 5-of-a-kind and 5-straights score. All other hands = 0. (Extreme)
- **Lone Wolf** — Only 1 equipment slot. It has permanent ghost aura.
- **No Re-rolls** — Exactly what it sounds like. Trail Rations becomes god-tier.
- **Spirit Run** — All dice start with random auras. No enhancements exist.

---

## Summary: Top 5 Highest-Impact Suggestions

If I had to pick the 5 that would most differentiate the game:

1. **Weather System** — Low implementation cost, high variety, extremely thematic
2. **Trail Events** — Narrative richness that Balatro completely lacks, makes every run a story
3. **Dice Crafting** — Unique to dice games, satisfying progression, creates "eureka" moments
4. **Bounty System** — Gives purpose to varied play, easy to add incrementally
5. **Formation Bonuses** — Uniquely suited to dice (not cards), adds positional strategy

These five together would make the game feel nothing like Balatro while keeping the satisfying core loop of "build collection → optimize scoring → beat escalating challenges."
