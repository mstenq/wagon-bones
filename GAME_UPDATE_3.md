# New Systems

## Dice system rework
We are removing the standard dice, they were boring and . Instead you start with bone and wood dice. Some professions may have additional dice like gold for banker, purple flower stickers for merchants, etc.  By default though each player will start with 30 wood dice, and 20 bone dice. 1 other big change is that there is no longer going to be a pick dice phase. You'll get your hand size worth of random dice (typically 8) and you have to make do with the 8 you get. Will probably have to add some equipment that can help discard x number of dice, but that can be later. I don't want to have a discard action and a reroll action as that seems like too much, so I like the idea of just getting 8 random dice. After you play a hand, whatever dice remained will still be in your hand just suplemented by additional dice. This way slag (See below) will feel more impactiful and you have to be careful about forging too many powerful dice cause you'll be clogging your collection with junk too.

## Existing Dice Changes
Remove blurry and loaded dice. Loaded dice may become a sticker. Blurry has too many game breaking consequences.

## DICE CRAFTING / COMBINING
**The Big Idea:** Two dice can be fused at a "Blacksmith" shop (appears every other leg in the shop) to create a more powerful hybrid die. It costs $5 to enter the workshop and you get to choose 2 of 8 randomly drawn dice from your active pool (Just like a booster pack). Each time you enter the workshop per shop costs an additional dollar (Kind of like shop rerolls).

**Rules:**
- Both dice are destroyed, one new die is created
- If both dice have a sticker, the new die takes the rights sticker.
- If both have auras, the new die takes the rights aura.
- Each combo gives a new dice type. You will not be told types before hand, so players have fun trying combos.

## Slag Dice
Forging dice can create slag dice. Each recipe has 1 to 3 chances of creating slag. Each chance is a (1 in 2) chance. 

Slag - clogs up collection, has no dice values, gives no mult or miles. Its like a stone dice that adds no miles.

## 3. TRAIL EVENTS (Between-Round Narrative Moments)

**The Big Idea:** After beating a leg but before the shop, a random trail event occurs. These are choose-your-own-adventure moments with risk/reward.

**Examples:**
- **"River Crossing"** — Pay $5 to ford safely, or risk it: 50% chance nothing happens, 30% lose a random enhanced die, 20% find a rare equipment
- **"Broken Wheel"** — Lose 1 day next round OR pay $8 to fix immediately
- **"Fellow Traveler"** — A stranger offers to trade: one of your equipment for a random uncommon+ equipment (sight unseen)
- **"Abandoned Wagon"** — Gain 3 random unenhanced dice, but next boss is upgraded (harder)
- **"Gold Strike!"** — Spend your next 2 rerolls (next round) to gain $15 now
- **"Sick Oxen"** — Lose $3 per day next round, OR sacrifice a die to cure them
- **"Native Guide"** — Offers 3 free trail guides of your choice, but you must skip the next shop
- **"Bandit Ambush"** — Lose half your money OR sacrifice an equipment to fight them off (gain $10 bounty)

**Why it works:** Adds narrative variety, tough decisions, and makes each run feel like a *story*. Deeply thematic. Also breaks up the rhythm of play→shop→play→shop.
